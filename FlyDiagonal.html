The "Theta Bomb" Strategy

A multi-leg options income strategy combining a Call Broken Wing Butterfly with a Put Diagonal Time Spread. The system culminates in its most advanced form: the 8-Leg "Fly Diagonal". Designed for high-probability income generation, leveraging extreme theta decay with defined risk.

Win Rate: ~94%
Profit Factor: 3.59
Timeframe: 4 - 14 DTE

The Core Setup (Base Flyagonal)

1. The Income Engine

Call Broken Wing Butterfly (BWB)

  • Placement: Positioned fully above the current spot price.
  • Structure: Standard 50/60 point wide wings (e.g., lower wing is 50pts, upper wing is 60pts wide).
  • Function: Generates strong positive Theta. This is where the majority of the profit is realized.
  • Risk: Defined risk, but creates a "tent" of profitability on the upside.

2. The Downside Anchor

Put Diagonal / Time Spread

  • Placement: Positioned fully below the current spot price.
  • Structure: Standard is a 10-point drop between strikes.
  • Time Gap: The short put typically expires 3 to 5 Days To Expiration (DTE) before the long put.
  • Function: Acts as low-cost insurance. Widens the profit range dramatically and handles downside float or volatility spikes.
Ideal Assets & Symbols

SPX is the primary vehicle due to liquidity, tight bid/ask spreads, and 1256 tax advantages (60% long-term / 40% short-term capital gains). Alternatives include SPY, QQQ, RUT, and IWM. Avoid standard corporate stocks, and never trade this strategy over earnings events (overnight gaps destroy the structure).

The 8-Leg "Fly Diagonal" Structure

While the standard Flyagonal uses 5 to 6 legs (a Call BWB + Put Diagonal), the strategy evolved into an advanced variant known explicitly as the Fly Diagonal. Described by Steve as a "big old spider," this 8-leg structure creates a highly resilient, "dagger-shaped" P&L diagram designed for outsized returns in extremely short timeframes (averaging just 4 days per trade).

The Dagger Shape

The 8-leg configuration manipulates the T+0 line to form a distinct "dagger shape," heavily expanding the profitability tent and smoothing out the "valley of death" found in simpler butterfly structures.

Unmatched Consistency

In its initial live market deployment, the 8-leg Fly Diagonal achieved an incredible 100% win rate across its first 50 consecutive trades, generating rapid capital turnover.

Software Dependent

Managing 8 simultaneous option legs is too complex for basic broker platforms. Advanced modeling tools like Option Traders Assistant are required to track combined Greeks, import trades, and execute unified adjustments.

Anatomy of the 8 Legs: By expanding the standard Flyagonal, this variant introduces additional staggered put and call structures (such as layering additional time spreads or breaking the Call BWB into wider, multi-cycle verticals) to entirely neutralize directional risk while hyper-accelerating theta decay.

Execution & Exit Rules

Profit Targets (PT)

  • Standard Target: 10% - 15% The primary goal is capturing rapid theta decay. Set automated limit orders to exit the entire strategy once the position hits a 10% to 15% return on capital at risk.
  • Quick Exit Rule (0-1 Days): > 4% If the trade achieves greater than a 4% profit within the first 24 hours (0-1 trading days), close it immediately. Take the fast capital and reallocate.
  • Trailing Stops (Advanced): For advanced management, allow profits to run to 20-30% while the market chops under the BWB tent, using a trailing stop to close the position if profit pulls back by a set percentage.

Stop Loss (SL) & Capital

  • Capital Allocation: Standard risk is roughly $2,500 per SPX trade. You must be comfortable having $200-$300 at risk per trade if scaling down to SPY. Max open trades are usually 3 to 4 staggered positions.
  • The Stop Loss Philosophy: Because losers can be 3 to 4 times the size of average winners, proactive adjustments are prioritized over hard stop-loss orders.
  • Lottery Ticket Rule: When exiting the trade, if a long leg (Long Call or Long Put) has decayed to just $1 or $2 in value, leave it open. It serves as a free "lottery ticket" against black swan events. Never leave a naked short open.

Trade Adjustments

Adjustments are made to flatten out deltas when skewed, keep positive Theta in play, and control overall buying power expansion.

Upside Market Adjustments

Triggered when the market rallies aggressively towards the BWB tent.

  • Flatten Deltas: Shift the Call BWB up the options chain or widen the wings to reduce negative delta.
  • Stay under the Tent: Ensure the current price remains under the BWB peak to maintain positive theta decay.
  • Rolling: Roll the entire structure forward in time if the spot price breaches the profitability tent and theta turns negative.

Downside Market Adjustments

Triggered when the market drops, testing the Put Diagonal insurance.

  • Evaluate Diagonal Support: The Put Diagonal should increase in value as volatility and price drop, offsetting BWB losses.
  • Shift the BWB: Bring the Call BWB closer to the money (downward) to capture more premium and re-center the tent over the new price.
  • Close Stragglers: Ensure that as you adjust, you are not inadvertently expanding your buying power or leaving short legs exposed.

Crucial Software Note: Adjustments on an 8-leg strategy require robust modeling. Tools like Option Traders Assistant or OptionStrat are highly recommended to accurately measure how adjustments affect your T+0 line and Theta. ThinkOrSwim can be used but requires manual leg importing.

Strategy Variants

Wide DTE Put Diagonal

Extends the Put Diagonal time gap from 3-5 days to 7-14 days.

Pros/Cons: Creates a much wider safety net (valley of death is much higher) and a higher profit tent. However, it requires significantly more out-of-pocket capital to open.

The Conagonal & Vertagonal

Alternative configurations discussed in the advanced sections, altering the BWB into Condor or Vertical spreads while maintaining the diagonal anchor.

When to use: Employed when implied volatility skew dictates that butterfly wings are too expensive, or when targeting a wider, flatter profit zone.
