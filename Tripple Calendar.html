Triple Calendar Spread

Setup Mechanics

The Underlying

Highly liquid indices are preferred. QQQ is the favorite, but SPY and SPX work exceptionally well. Individual stocks near earnings should be avoided unless highly experienced.

Timeframes (DTE)

Typically entered on Fridays.
Sell Leg: 21 Days to Expiration (DTE).
Buy Leg: 28 Days to Expiration (DTE - One week after the sell leg).

Strike Selection Rules

  • Middle Calendar: At-The-Money (ATM), rounded to the nearest multiple of 5 (e.g., if price is 638, use 635 or 640).
  • Determine Expected Move: Look at the 21 DTE ATM Straddle price (Call + Put price combined). E.g., 25 points.
  • Upper Calendar: ATM Strike + Expected Move + ~5 points margin. (e.g., 635 + 25 + 5 = 665).
  • Lower Calendar: ATM Strike - Expected Move - ~5 points margin. (e.g., 635 - 25 - 5 = 605).

Exit Rules

Take Profit

Strictly take profit at 10% of the initial debit paid for the spreads. Do not get greedy. Close the entire trade.

Stop Loss (Hard Time Stop)

No percentage stop loss. Instead, use a hard time limit. You must close the trade exactly when 7 days remain until the short leg's expiration (which is exactly 14 days in the trade). Cut it regardless of P/L.

Important Rules

  • Avoid Binary Events

    Never sell a short leg directly ahead of FOMC meetings, CPI releases, or major earnings. Ensure events fall *between* your short and long expirations if possible.

  • Positive Vega Strategy

    This strategy benefits from rising implied volatility (VIX going up). If VIX falls heavily, profit targets will take longer to hit.

  • Adjustments (Advanced)

    If the underlying blows past your upper or lower strikes, you can add a 4th calendar spread further out. However, sticking to the 7-day hard stop is usually safer for beginners.
