The HV7 Option Trading System

Workshop Reference Guide by Amy Meissner

SPX Options Market Neutral High Volatility (VIX) 93% Win Rate

Overview: The HV7 is a very short-term, high-probability market-neutral options strategy (Broken Wing Butterfly) traded on the weeklies to take advantage of quick theta decay. It strictly targets specific high-volatility events as a signal to enter. There are no adjustments made to this trade.

1. The Signal (Trigger) & Timing

Entry Criteria

Both of the following must happen during regular market hours on the same day (though not necessarily at the exact same time):

  • SPX must have been DOWN at least 2%.
  • VIX must have hit a level of at least 27.

Trade Timing

  • • Enter the trade about 10-15 minutes before market close.
  • • Use the Friday Expiration with at least 7 DTE.
  • • Exception: If entry triggers on a Monday, an additional trade with a 7 DTE Monday expiration can be added.
  • • Skip if there is no Friday expiration (e.g., market holiday).

2. Position Setups (BWB using Puts)

Set up a Broken Wing Butterfly (BWB) using PUTS with very flat delta (approx -0.50 to +0.20 for a 1 lot).

Standard Entry (ST)

  • Upper Long Strike: ATM (~50 Delta)
  • Short Strikes (2x): ~35 Delta handle
  • Lower Long Strike: ~20 Delta (or whatever is needed to flatten deltas)
Planned Capital: $5,000 | Avg Margin: ~$2,800

Low Margin Entry

  • Upper Long Strike: ATM (~50 Delta)
  • Short & Lower Strikes: Find strikes that allow for no more than a 10 point spread difference between the upper (debit) portion and the lower (credit) portion of the BWB.
Planned Capital: $1,500 | Avg Margin: ~$1,200

3. Profit Targets (PT) & Exits

You can set Good 'Til Canceled (GTC) orders right after entry. There are NO adjustments made to this trade.

Profit Targets (PT)

  • Standard PT: 5% of the margin used.
  • Low Margin PT: 5% of margin, but lower to 3% of margin if the market is above the upper wing strike of the BWB.

Exit Rules

  • • Exit the trade if position Theta becomes negative while the market is above the upper wing strike of the BWB. (Ignore if below the lower wing).
  • • Expiration Rule: Exit the trade before expiration day. Do NOT stay in the trade during expiration day!
Max Loss: There is no official max loss rule. The statistical nature of the large entry requirements heavily favors a bounce if SPX drops below the lower wing. However, personal risk tolerance applies.

4. Additional Notes & Definitions

  • RUT Alternative The Russell 2000 (RUT) index can also be used. The entry trigger requires RUT to be down 2% (instead of SPX). All other VIX rules, entry, and exit parameters remain the same. (SPX is preferred for liquidity and taxes).
  • Handle In option terms as referenced in the workshop, a "handle" refers to the specific strike price of the option. (e.g. "a 35 delta handle" means the strike closest to a 35 delta).
  • "Under the Tent" Refers to the profit zone of a butterfly position graph at expiration. Being under the tent means the trade is in the profitable area.
  • T+ Lines Theoretical P/L at different days in the future (T+0 = today, T+1 = tomorrow, etc).
